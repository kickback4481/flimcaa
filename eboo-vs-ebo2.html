Understanding the Difference: EBOO vs EBO₂

What Is the Difference Between EBOO and EBO₂?

Both EBOO (Extracorporeal Blood Oxygenation & Ozonation) and EBO₂ (Extracorporeal Blood Oxygenation & Ozonation) are advanced, physician-guided wellness therapies that circulate blood outside the body through a specialized medical system where it is exposed to medical-grade oxygen and ozone before being returned to the bloodstream. Both therapies are designed to support healthy circulation, oxygen utilization, immune function, and overall physiological wellness as part of a personalized integrative care plan.

While the therapies share many similarities, they differ in their design, filtration capabilities, blood-processing volume, and overall intensity.

EBOO

EBOO is an advanced ozone wellness therapy that continuously circulates blood through a specialized extracorporeal system where it is oxygenated, exposed to medical-grade ozone, and returned to the body. It is designed to support healthy circulation, oxygen delivery, cellular wellness, and the body’s natural detoxification processes. EBOO provides comprehensive ozone and oxygen support while processing a significant volume of blood during each physician-guided session.

EBO₂

EBO₂ builds upon these same core principles while incorporating an advanced high-performance blood filtration system. As blood continuously circulates through the extracorporeal circuit, it is filtered, oxygenated, and precisely exposed to medical-grade ozone before being returned to the body. This additional filtration component is designed to help reduce the burden of unwanted particles that may accumulate in the bloodstream while supporting healthy circulation, oxygen delivery, and overall physiological balance.

Key Differences

  • Filtration: EBO₂ incorporates advanced blood filtration as part of the procedure, while traditional EBOO focuses primarily on oxygenation and ozonation.
  • Blood Processing: EBO₂ is designed to process a larger volume of blood through a continuous filtration circuit during the session.
  • Technology: EBO₂ utilizes a larger, more advanced extracorporeal filtration system capable of combining filtration, oxygenation, and ozonation into one continuous process.
  • Personalization: Both therapies are individualized and performed under physician supervision using customized protocols based on each patient’s health history, wellness goals, and overall care plan.

Which Therapy Is Right for You?

Neither therapy is considered universally better than the other. Each offers unique advantages, and the most appropriate option depends on your individual health history, wellness objectives, physician evaluation, and personalized wellness plan.
